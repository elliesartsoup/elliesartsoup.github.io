  1. Prepare Clay: Gather clay and knead it to remove air bubbles.
  2. Shape: Mold clay into desired shape, using hands or pottery tools.
  3. Dry: Let the shaped clay air dry until it's leather hard.
  4. Bisque Firing: Fire the dried clay in a kiln at a low temperature (around 1800°F or 980°C) to remove moisture and make it sturdy.
  5. Glaze: Apply desired glaze to the bisque-fired piece.
  6. Glaze Firing: Fire the glazed piece in the kiln at a higher temperature (around 2200°F or 1200°C) to fuse the glaze and clay together.
  7. Cooling: Allow the ceramics to cool slowly in the kiln to prevent cracking.
  8. Enjoy: Your homemade ceramics are ready to use or display!
